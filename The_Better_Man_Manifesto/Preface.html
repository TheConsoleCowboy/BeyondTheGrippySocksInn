What the hell is The Better Man Manifesto?... An Explanation

This is in no way, shape, or form an attempt to claim these to be the "ideal standards for manhood" or anything of the sort. When I say, "Better Man," I mean striving to be a better man today than who I was yesterday.

The inspiration for the name of this page honestly comes from that Taylor Swift song:

"Sometimes, in the middle of the night, I can feel you again
But I just miss you and I just wish you were a better man.""

See, I started this whole project as a way of keeping myself afloat when I was awake at 3 A.M. missing someone very dear to me who, let's face it, left because I didn't know how to be grateful for the good thing we had without being terrified about it being less than "100% safe." And... well,
I write a lot of the pages on this site wishing I had been that better man.

This is the space where I plan to take the work I have been doing on Obsidian (discussed sometimes in my living journal) and make sense of what it is to be a "better man." And, in time, I will pair what exists on here with references to either academic or populare press writings; back up the ideas I have with the voices of those whose inputs actually carry some weight,
or find that there is a different perspective I should be taking altogether.

After all, these things are iterative, no?

So, all said, all done, all prefaced out, without further ado:
